Welcome to the first ever Two Tap newsletter.

We've been working non-stop in the background to provide you the tools to create delightful experiences for your shoppers. Since this is the first ever update it's going to be full to interesting tid-bits, so settle in.

575 Retailers

We launched with 30 live retailers. That was easy. Since then we've grown to support 12x that number, and we've streamlined the process. Our integration team can now add 75 to 100 new retailers every month.

More than that, we are keeping up with all the innevitable retailer website changes, and have become more stable than ever....

We've become more reliable than the retailer websites

Mid last year we switched from live order placing (where the shoppers had to wait for confirmation), to an asynchronous process where the orders are placed in the background. Since this was a new flow we had no idea how our shoppers would react, but we decided to take a shot and the result was a complete success.

Right now we take an order, save its information, and let the shopper go about her routine. In the background we then place the order on the retailer's website. This process usually takes 3-5 minutes, at which point we (or you) send the shopper a confirmation. The retailer website is in fact the most reliable piece of their infrastructure. If a store's website goes down so do their sales.

Because of this, API wise, we are slowly switching all our integrations from a polling model (where you ping /status) to callbacks (where we make an request to your servers when an order is being placed).

This ensures we *never lose a sale*. Even if the retailer website goes down (90% of our issues are not integrations breaking, but retailer websites being unavailable or buggy), we save the information and place it when the store comes back up.

24/7 monitoring of integrations and orders

We have an amazing oncall team that's always monitoring your orders. Whatever happens, whether it's an integration breaking, an order with weird input, an edge case, or anything that prevents an order from being placed, you can be sure that someone is on it.

Shipping options and secondary images

Two Tap already supported different images for different colors on each product. We now return all the available product images instead of just one per color. In the HTML5 interface you can now zoom and see images fullscreen.

Shipping options are incredibly important and we always wondered how we can build a universal system that could support thousands of stores in one clean API. We've come up with a simple but effective solution: we offer shoppers two shipping methods, cheapest or fastest. This has proven to be incredibly effective and it's available on the majority of our retailers.

Our fast iterations speak for the flexibility of Two Tap. Once the backend was ready, our retailer team was able to add secondary images support for 550+ retailers in five days. It would have been impossible to ask 550+ retailers to add a new feature to their APIs in this time frame.
